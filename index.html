How It Works

Four peer-reviewed steps turn your usage into real-world numbers.

01

Wh per Query

We use officially disclosed figures where available: OpenAI confirmed 0.34 Wh/query (Altman, June 2025), Google reported 0.24 Wh/query for Gemini (August 2025). Undisclosed models use independent academic benchmarks.

02

Data Centre Overhead

Data centres need extra energy for cooling beyond the AI chips themselves. We apply Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE): 1.10 for Google/hyperscalers, 1.40 for Chinese data centres — based on Uptime Institute 2024 survey data.

03

Carbon Intensity

The same electricity produces very different CO₂ depending on the country. US: 0.386 kg/kWh, EU: 0.256, China: 0.555 (IEA 2024). This is why DeepSeek's lower energy use can still produce high emissions — China's grid is coal-heavy.

04

Real-world Equivalences

CO₂ grams are converted into things you already understand: car trips, trees, phone charges. Tree offset rate: 21.8 kg CO₂/year (US EPA). Car emissions: 0.21 kg CO₂/km (European average, EEA 2024).

Reduce Your Impact

Small changes in how you use AI can cut your carbon footprint significantly.

🎯

Write Clearer Prompts

A concise, specific prompt gets a better answer faster and uses fewer tokens — directly cutting the energy cost without sacrificing quality.

🔄

Don't Regenerate Needlessly

Every regeneration roughly doubles the energy spent. Think before clicking "Try again" — refine your prompt instead.

📦

Batch Your Questions

Sending 10 short messages uses more energy than one well-structured prompt covering all your questions at once.

🌱

Choose an Efficient Model

Gemini currently has the lowest officially reported per-query footprint (0.03 g CO₂). For simple tasks, smaller models use far less energy than frontier ones.

💡

Ask Yourself: Does This Need AI?

A Google search uses ~0.3 Wh — roughly equal to Gemini's efficiency today. For simple factual lookups, a search engine may be just as fast.

🌳

Offset What You Can't Cut

Gold Standard and Pachama offer verified carbon offsets funding real reforestation. CodeCarbon.io can track emissions from your AI coding projects.
